Allison McDonald

I am a first year PhD student working with Professor J. Alex Halderman at the University of Michigan.

My research interests lie in the intersection of technology and society, particularly in the areas of privacy and security. I study problems such as Internet censorship, mass surveillance, and anonymity systems. I am also interested in exploring the disparity in understanding between the developers and users of technology, and the consequences this disparity has on how technology is used, perceived, and legislated.

I am a former Roger M Jones Fellow at the European University Viadrina, where my experiences were chronicled on the fellowship blog.
